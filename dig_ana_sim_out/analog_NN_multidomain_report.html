Diagnosis: Pervasive Op-Amp Clipping FAIL

ObservationEvery tested (codes, Vin) combination produces Vout ≈ 3.25-3.26 V — the TLV9061 positive rail (3.3 V) with ~50 mV headroom.
Root causeThe TIA feedback resistor (Rf) is extremely large relative to the AD5160 wiper resistance (Rw). Even Rw_min (code=0) with Vin=1 mV produces an ideal output >160 V, far exceeding the 3.3 V supply.
ImplicationThe crossbar operates in hard saturation for ALL practical inputs. The MVM is non-functional as a linear computation — it only outputs rail voltage.
Fix neededReduce Rf by 100–1000×, or reduce input voltage range, or increase VCC (TLV9061 supports up to 5.5 V) but fundamental gain is still too high.
